About Ryan Saperstein

My Programming Experience

I started programming when I was in eighth grade on my middle school's robotics team, I and continued to program throughout high school on my school's FIRST Robotics Competition team. However, I quickly became interested in programming applications outside of robotics, so I joined my school's Code Club and was able to work on many different projects, from creating snowflakes out of LED lights that turned on an off to designing mobile apps. After graduating high school, I started studying computer science at Northeastern University, where I learned principles of object oriented and functional program design.

I am most proficient in Java and Python, but also have experience in C, C++, Racket, and JavaScript. I have experience with GitHub, Docker, and Linux (mainly Ubuntu). I've both worked in and led teams of programmers on a range of projects, from programming robots to creating mobile apps.
