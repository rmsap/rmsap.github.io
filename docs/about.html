About Ryan Saperstein

Originally from central New Jersey, I'm now a second year computer science student at Northeastern University. In class, I've learned principles of both object-oriented and functional programming design. I am most proficient in Java and Python, but also have experience in C, C++, Racket, and JavaScript. I've both worked in and led teams of programmers on a range of projects, from programming robots to creating mobile apps.

I started programming when I was in eighth grade and continued to program throughout high school on my school's FIRST Robotics Competition team. However, I quickly became interested in programming applications outside of robotics, so I joined my school's Code Club and became Co-President in tenth grade. Through many different projects, I became the programmer that I am today.

Outside of programming, I enjoy reading and playing video games. My favorite books are Frankenstein by Mary Shelley and We by Yevgeny Zamyatin. I also like to read other sci-fi and fantasy books. I also like to play wiffle ball and go rock climbing with my friends.
